<h1>🌍 Africa Sovereign Debt Analysis</h1>

<h2>📌 Overview</h2>
<p>
This project presents a data-driven analysis of Africa’s sovereign debt crisis, examining fiscal trends, debt pressures, and economic performance across multiple countries from 2014 to 2024.
</p>

<p>
Developed during a global data hackathon, the analysis explores how government spending, revenue patterns, interest rates, and economic growth contribute to rising debt levels and fiscal instability.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>🌍 Why This Matters</h2>
<p>
Public debt across African economies has grown rapidly, often outpacing economic growth and increasing pressure on public finances. Rising debt servicing costs are limiting investment in critical sectors such as healthcare, education, and infrastructure.
</p>

<p>
Understanding these dynamics is essential for designing sustainable fiscal policies and improving long-term economic stability.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>🎯 Objectives</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Analyze fiscal trends across African countries</li>
  <li>Examine the relationship between revenue, expenditure, and debt</li>
  <li>Identify indicators of financial stress and instability</li>
  <li>Provide data-driven insights for policy and economic sustainability</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h2>📊 Scope of Analysis</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Countries:</strong> 14 African economies</li>
  <li><strong>Time period:</strong> 2014–2024</li>
  <li><strong>Key indicators:</strong></li>
  <ul>
    <li>Budget deficit / surplus</li>

    <li>GDP growth and real GDP</li>

  </ul>
</ul>

<h2>🔍 Key Analysis</h2>

<h3>1. Fiscal Trends</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Identified rising deficits across multiple countries</li>
  <li>Highlighted economies with the largest fiscal deterioration</li>
</ul>

<h3>2. Revenue vs Expenditure</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Compared growth in government revenue and spending</li>
  <li>Identified fiscal pressure gaps where spending outpaced revenue</li>
</ul>

<h3>3. Economic Growth & Capacity</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Analyzed GDP trends and production capacity</li>
  <li>Identified high-growth and low-growth economies</li>
</ul>

<h3>4. Financial Conditions</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Examined interest rates and borrowing conditions</li>
  <li>Assessed implications for investment and economic activity</li>
</ul>

<h3>5. Structural Pressures</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Analyzed unemployment trends across countries</li>
  <li>Explored climate-related economic risks</li>
</ul>

<h2>📈 Key Insights</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Debt is rising faster than economic growth across many countries</li>
  <li>Spending growth consistently outpaces revenue in several economies</li>
  <li>High interest rates are tightening financial conditions</li>
  <li>Economic growth remains uneven across the region</li>
  <li>Climate shocks are increasing fiscal pressure</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h2>📌 Recommendations</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Strengthen revenue mobilisation in low-performing economies</li>
  <li>Reduce fiscal gaps between spending and income</li>
  <li>Monitor and manage interest rate impacts</li>
  <li>Improve data systems for cross-country learning</li>
</ul>
